Pitch: Hiring me at Basecamp

By Jason Taylor, Future Developer at Basecamp

My curiosity for writing code started my senior year of college, during an internship at a telecommunications company. I was working on a sales tool that configured large phone systems with technical accuracy. I realized, early on in this experience, that I wanted to fully immerse myself in the world of software. This was a craft that I had passion for and was excited at the opportunity develop an expertise.

To become a master at this craft, I am constantly challenging myself to expand my knowledge and skills. This means I am committed to a lifetime of learning, experimenting, and improving. It means I am never satisfied with just "good enough." Additionally, I recognize the benefit of learning from other masters in order to increase my understanding. More specifically, Basecamp would be an unparalleled opportunity for me to join a community of experts in software.

As a team member, I am deeply passionate about building high quality products with the kind of care and attention to detail that is crucial. I'm someone who values learning new technologies but who also values the stability of well developed and well tested systems that have been proven to work. Oh, and my entire group of friends think I'm fun to be around!

Moreover, I am a well-rounded team player. Thanks to my dad, who ran an auto repair business for 30 years, I grew up in the small business world. Thus, I understand the need to be versatile and flexible in order to make a business successful. For instance, a company may not have the luxury to hire someone for a specific task or problem but it still must be solved. My standard of work is to contribute in any way I can, regardless of what is outlined in my job description.

At my current position, I was recently asked which part of the system I wanted to work on and I immediately replied, "everything." I always try to learn how every part of a system works. That is, understanding the system holistically, allows me to better solve the problem(s). This has been especially important to me when working on small teams, because with this knowledge I can be a leader who helps others gain a similar perspective.

One of the things I respect most about Basecamp is your commitment to solving the problems that arise when teams of people try to work together. In my experience, teams struggle most when there isn't clear communication. Consequently, this results in frustrated team members and lower quality output. Basecamp, the product, provides a single source of truth by consolidating team communications to fewer channels. Basecamp, the people, provide an amazing counterbalance to the normal industry standard of how companies are supposed to operate. I would love to join and help spread these ideals.

Code Examples

My Original Rails App

Code on GitHub

This is the first Rails app I ever built. I play in a fantasy football league with some friends that has a very complex ruleset. We were originally managing it with email and spreadsheets which proved to be prohibitively cumbersome so a friend and I started this app to manage team rosters, weekly lineups, and scoring.

It started out life on Rails 3.0 but is currently on 4.2 and now features live scoring of games during the season. It hasn't had a ton of upgrades recently but runs here flawlessy each NFL season.

Parsing Library for NFL Stats

Code on GitHub | RubyGems page

This gem is the parsing library that we use for the fantasy football app. It scrapes several pages of NFL.com to gather weekly statistics, information about players, and team schedules. I extracted this gem from the overall application a few years ago because I thought people might find it useful.

CardConnect API Ruby Client

Code on GitHub | RubyGems page

This gem is a client API for interacting with the CardConnect payment gateway I built this for my current company, MobileCause, to be enable our system to process donations through this payment gateway. CardConnect doesn't support Ruby specifically so I built this with the Ruby community in mind.
